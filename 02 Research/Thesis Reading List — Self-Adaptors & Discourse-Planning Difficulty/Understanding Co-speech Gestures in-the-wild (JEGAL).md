**Background.**  gesture understanding lacked in-the-wild, task-rich evaluation. 

**Research question / methodology.**  JEGAL, a tri-modal (gesture/audio/language) joint embedding trained with a global phrase-level contrastive objective and a local gesture-word coupling objective; new tasks for retrieval, gestured-word spotting, and gesture-based active-speaker detection. 

**Conclusion.**  strong cross-modal correspondence learning -- a pretraining template for gesture-like motion embeddings.
