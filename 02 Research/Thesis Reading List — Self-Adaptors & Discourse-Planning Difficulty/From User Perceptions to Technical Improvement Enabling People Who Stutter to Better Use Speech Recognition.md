Recommended reading, found via the reference list of Romana, Koishida & Mower Provost (2024), "Automatic Disfluency Detection From Untranscribed Speech" (IEEE/ACM TASLP). Large-scale (61-person survey + 91-person study) HCI investigation of how people who stutter are hurt by voice-assistant/dictation ASR cutting them off or misreading disfluent speech, with technical fixes reducing premature cutoffs by 79.1% and WER from 25.4% to 9.9%. Directly complements the TASLP paper's finding that ASR transcript quality bottlenecks disfluency detection.
